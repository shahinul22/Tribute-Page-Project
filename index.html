Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, the founding leader of Bangladesh, led the nation to independence in 1971 and is honored as the "Father of the Nation" for his enduring legacy. Below is a timeline of major events in their life:

<
  • Born: March 17, 1920
  • Major Achievement 1: Led the historic 7th March Speech in 1971, calling for independence.
  • Major Achievement 2: First President of Bangladesh after its independence in 1971.
  • Died: August 15, 1975
  • Major Achievement 3: Instrumental in forming the Awami League in 1949, advocating for Bengali rights.
  • Major Achievement 4: Played a key role in the Six-Point Movement in 1966, demanding greater autonomy for East Pakistan.
  • Legacy: Honored as the "Father of the Nation" for his leadership and vision.
  • Posthumous Recognition: Awarded the Gaddafi International Prize for Human Rights in 1989.
